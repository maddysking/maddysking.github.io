The Case of JonBenét Ramsey

Timeline of Events
  • December 25, 1996: The Ramsey’s celebrated Christmas in their normal fashion. They opened presents, enjoyed the day together, and attended the White’s annual Christmas Party. The Ramsey’s claim that JonBenet fell asleep on the car ride home from the party and that John immediately put her to bed. According to the Ramsey’s they went to bed shortly after in preparation for an early morning flight. John Ramsey claims he took a sleeping pill and was never woken during the night. Patsy and Burke both claim they slept through the night as well.
  • December 26, 1996: Patsy Ramsey was the first person to wake up, she went down her spiral stair case and found a three page ransom note sprawled out across the bottom step. She claims to have checked JonBenet’s room and woken John.
    • 5:52am: Patsy called 911.
    • 5:54am: Patsy called the Whites.
    • 5:59am The first officer arrives on the scene.
    • Sometime after 6:00am:Family friends arrive. Followed by crime scene investigators and victim advocates.
    • 8:10am: Detective Linda Arndt arrives at the Ramsey house.
    • 8:15am: Ron Walker is contacted by the Boulder P.D. (FBI)
    • **10:00am: They were supposed to get a call from kidnappers, but it never came.
    • 10:30am: Detective Arndt notices that John Ramsey has disappeared.
    • 12:00pm: Detective Arndt notifies her superiors that John is still missing.
    • 1:00pm: John Ramsey and Fleet White search the house again from “top to bottom” under Arndt’s instructions. Although being told to begin in the upstairs, John Ramsey makes a beeline for the basement.
    • 1:05pm: John Ramsey discovers JonBenet’s body in the “wine cellar” and carries her body upstairs.
    • 1:10pm: Detective Arndt moves the body again.
  • December 30, 1996: The Boulder police annouce that they have taken DNA samples from John and Patsy Ramsey as well as other family members.
  • December 31, 1996: JonBenet is buried in Marietta, Georgia. John and Patsy Ramsey also hire their own investigators, attorneys, and a publisist.
  • January 1, 1997: John and Patsy Ramsey appear on CNN to give their first public interview.
  • January 22, 1997: It is reported that the Ramsey family refuse to submit polygraph tests.
  • February 12, 1997: The Ramsey’s refuse to comply with the police conditions of interviews.
  • March 13, 1997: Retired detective, Lou Smit, joins the investigative team. He heavily supported and endorsed the intruder theory.
  • April 30, 1997: The Ramseys submit to their first formal police interview since the murder. They are interviewed separately.
  • June 27, 1997: Having cleared John Ramsey as the author of the ransom note 2 months prior, the Colorodo Bureau of Investigation take their fifth handwiritng sample from Patsy Ramsey and continue to deem the results “inconclusive.”
  • July 1997 to 1999: The grand jury hear evidence, but disbands without an indictment in October of 1999.
  • July 2008: The Ramsey’s are told by Boulder’s District Attorney’s office that they are no longer considered suspects after new technology allows touch DNA samples to be further examined.
  • August 2008: John Mark Karr confesses to the murder of JonBenet, but is released after evidence proves he was not even in Colorado at the time of the murder.
  • February 2009: The investigation is reopened by Boulder Police.
  • January 2013: Information surfaces that proves the grand jury voted to indict John and Patsy Ramsey back in 1999, but they were never indicted due to the D.A.’s refusal to sign.
  • December 26, 2016: The 20 year anniversary of jonBenet’s murder sparks a resurgence of media coverage on the case in the months prior to the anniversary.
